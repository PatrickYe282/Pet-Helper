What To Do If Your Pet Is Missing

1

Don't wait. Start searching your immediate neighborhood within the first 10 minutes.

2

Post on social media right now. Share a clear photo, your pet's name, last seen location, and your phone number.

3

Tell your neighbors in person. Knock on doors within a 3-block radius — most pets are found close to home.

4

Contact local shelters. Call or visit every shelter within 10 miles and leave a photo with staff.

5

Put up flyers. Place them at eye level near where your pet was last seen, especially at intersections.

How To Help a Neighbor's Lost Pet

Share the post. One share can reach hundreds of people in your neighborhood instantly.

Check your yard and garage. Lost pets often hide in quiet, sheltered spots nearby.

Look carefully — don't chase. A scared pet may run further if you rush toward it. Crouch down and speak softly.

Take a photo if you spot the pet. Even if you can't catch it, a location update helps the owner find them fast.

Quick Quiz — Test Your Knowledge

Myth vs. Fact

Myth"If my pet is missing, they'll find their way home on their own."▼
Fact: Most lost pets don't find their way home alone. Without a community post, they often end up in shelters — where cats have only a 2% chance of being reclaimed without ID.
Myth"Shelters will contact me if they find my pet."▼
Fact: Shelters are overwhelmed. You need to proactively visit and call every shelter within 10 miles — don't wait for them to call you.
Myth"Chasing a lost pet is the fastest way to catch them."▼
Fact: Chasing a scared pet almost always makes them run further. Crouch down, speak softly, and let them come to you.
Myth"Lost pets are usually found far from home."▼
Fact: Most lost pets are found within 2–3 miles of home. Start your search and your posts in your immediate neighborhood first.
